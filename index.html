About Us

Epilepsy — it’s something most people have heard of, but few truly understand. Unlike brain-bending topics like quantum mechanics or the federal tax code, epilepsy is intensely real and surprisingly common: over 3.4 million people in the U.S. live with it. That’s roughly 1 in 100 Americans who will experience it in their lifetime.

Spike Lab is a multidisciplinary team of researchers and engineers based at the Cleveland Clinic Epilepsy Center. From signal processing and computational modeling to AI and machine learning (fueled by coffee and GPUs), we build tools to decode neurological activity, analyze epileptic seizure, and simulate the brain’s complex dynamics. By combining deep clinical insight with engineering innovation, we’re designing practical, human-centered solutions for one of medicine’s most complex neurological disorders.

Our Research

AI-Based Seizure Prediction

Predicting seizures before they occur is one of the most ambitious challenges in epilepsy research — and we’re actively investigating how artificial intelligence can help address it. By analyzing intracranial EEG signals, our researchers, John and Sarah, are training machine learning models aimed at identifying subtle neural patterns that may precede a seizure. Leveraging time-frequency representations and deep learning architectures, they are working toward estimating seizure likelihood minutes in advance. While still in development, this research is laying the groundwork for predictive tools that could eventually provide patients with earlier warnings and greater control over their condition.

Highlighted Publications

AI-Based Seizure Prediction (Doe, et al., 2025)

This study presents a machine learning framework for forecasting epileptic seizures using intracranial EEG (iEEG) recordings. By extracting time-frequency features from multi-channel iEEG data, the researchers trained deep neural networks to distinguish pre-ictal (before seizure) states from interictal (non-seizure) activity. The model was evaluated on real patient data and demonstrated promising early warning capabilities, offering seizure risk predictions several minutes in advance. The work highlights the potential of AI-driven tools to support personalized seizure forecasting and improve quality of life for individuals with drug-resistant epilepsy.
